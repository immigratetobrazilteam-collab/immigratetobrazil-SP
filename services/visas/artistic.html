Frequently Asked Questions

Answers based on current regulations and real applicant experiences in S√£o Paulo.

How much money do I really need?

Minimum for S√£o Paulo:

  • First month expenses: R$3,000-6,000
  • Housing deposit: R$2,400-6,000 (3 months)
  • Emergency fund: R$5,000-10,000
  • Document/registration costs: R$1,000-2,000
  • Recommended total: R$15,000-30,000

Most immigrants underestimate first-year costs by 40-60%.

What if my application is denied?

Denials happen. Common reasons:

  • Incomplete documentation
  • Insufficient financial proof
  • Immigration history concerns
  • Application inconsistencies

Options: Fix issues and reapply (usually after 6 months). Application fees are non-refundable.

Do I need to speak Portuguese?

Legal requirement: No language test for most visas.

Practical reality: You need Portuguese for daily life in S√£o Paulo. English is limited outside tourist areas.

Recommendation: Start learning now. Budget for 3-6 months intensive classes after arrival.

Can I work while processing?

Depends on your visa type:

  • Work visa holders: May work after Federal Police registration
  • Tourist visa: No work allowed
  • Student visa: Limited work with restrictions

‚ö†Ô∏è Unauthorized work = deportation risk

What happens if I overstay?

Consequences:

  • Daily fines (R$8.28/day, increases yearly)
  • Possible detention and deportation
  • Entry bans (2-10 years)
  • Future visa complications globally

If you've overstayed: Contact Federal Police immediately to minimize consequences.

Need a lawyer or consultant?

Recommended if:

  • Complex case (previous denials, etc.)
  • Not fluent in Portuguese
  • Work/investment visas
  • Can't afford denial

Cost: R$3,000-15,000 depending on complexity

Often pays for itself by preventing denials.
