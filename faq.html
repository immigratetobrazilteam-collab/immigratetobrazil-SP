Frequently Asked Questions

Immigration to São Paulo, Brazil

Find answers to common questions about visa types, requirements, costs, and the immigration process to São Paulo.

General Immigration Questions

What are the main visa categories available?+

Brazil offers multiple visa categories including:

  • Work Visas (VITEM): For professionals and technical workers
  • Residency Permits: Permanent and temporary residence options
  • Family Visas: For spouses, dependents, and relatives
  • Student Visas (VITEM IV): For academic study
  • Artist Visas (VIART): For cultural and artistic professionals
  • Business Visa (VITEM II): For business meetings and conferences
  • Investor Visas: For real estate and business investment
What is the processing time for a visa application?+

Processing times vary by visa type and complexity:

  • Work Visas: 30-60 days
  • Residency Permits: 45-90 days
  • Family Visas: 30-45 days
  • Student Visas: 15-30 days
  • Artist Visas: 30-45 days

Complex cases may take longer. We'll provide an estimated timeline after reviewing your application.

What documents do I need to prepare?+

Required documents vary by visa type, but generally include:

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Completed visa application form
  • Proof of financial means
  • Background/police clearance certificate
  • Photographs (specific format required)
  • Proof of accommodation in Brazil
  • Health certificate (for some visa types)
  • Job offer or sponsorship letter (for work visas)

We provide a complete checklist tailored to your visa type during consultation.

How much does the visa application cost?+

Visa fees vary by type and nationality:

  • Work Visas: USD 100-300
  • Residency Permits: Varies
  • Family Visas: USD 100-200
  • Student Visas: Free to USD 100
  • Artist Visas: USD 100-200

These are consular fees only. Professional services may add to the total cost. Get a detailed quote after your consultation.

Can I work in Brazil on a tourist visa?+

No, working on a tourist visa is illegal in Brazil. You must obtain the appropriate work visa (VITEM) before accepting employment. Violations can result in fines, deportation, and legal penalties. If you wish to work, we can help you apply for the correct visa category.

How do I apply for permanent residency?+

Permanent residency in Brazil can be obtained through several paths:

  • Marriage to a Brazilian citizen
  • Family relationships (parents, children)
  • Retirement with sufficient funds
  • Real estate investment
  • Temporary residency conversion (typically after 4 years)

Each path has specific requirements. Contact us for details on your eligibility.

Work Visa Questions

What qualifications do I need for a work visa?+

Work visa requirements depend on the specific VITEM category:

  • VITEM I: Isolated activities (freelancers, consultants)
  • VITEM II: Business activities
  • VITEM III: Technical services and specialized skills

Generally, you'll need a job offer, professional qualifications, and proof that your skills are needed in Brazil. We'll evaluate your profile.

Do I need a job offer before applying for a work visa?+

Yes, a job offer or sponsorship letter from a Brazilian employer is typically required for work visas. The employer must submit a labor application confirming that foreign workers are needed for the position. We can guide you and your employer through this process.

Can I bring my family on a work visa?+

Yes! You can bring your spouse and dependent children on family visas. They can be included in your application or apply separately. Family members will need to provide their own documentation but won't need separate employment.

What is the salary requirement for a work visa?+

There is no fixed minimum salary requirement for work visas. However, your salary should be competitive and appropriate for your position and experience. The employer must justify why a foreign worker is necessary for the role.

Family & Residency Questions

Can I bring my spouse to Brazil?+

Yes! Spouses and stable partners can obtain visas through family reunification. You'll need:

  • Marriage certificate or proof of stable partnership
  • Proof of financial support
  • Valid passport
  • Police clearance

Same-sex marriages are recognized in Brazil, and spouses have equal rights.

Can I bring my children?+

Yes! Dependent children can be included in your visa application. You'll need:

  • Birth certificates
  • Proof of custody if applicable
  • Parental consent if only one parent is immigrating

Children will receive the same visa as you and can attend Brazilian schools.

Can I retire in Brazil?+

Yes! Brazil offers residency for retirees with sufficient income. You'll typically need:

  • Proof of monthly income (USD 2,000-3,000 depending on program)
  • Valid passport
  • Police clearance
  • Health certificate

Retirees can enjoy a comfortable lifestyle in São Paulo with relatively low living costs.

Student Visa Questions

What are the requirements for a student visa?+

Student visa (VITEM IV) requirements include:

  • Acceptance letter from a recognized Brazilian educational institution
  • Proof of financial means to support your studies
  • Valid passport
  • Police clearance
  • Academic records/transcripts
  • Health certificate

We can assist with university applications and visa processing.

Can I work while studying?+

International students can work in certain circumstances, typically:

  • On-campus work at the university
  • Internships related to your study program
  • Part-time work with employer sponsorship

Work must not interfere with your studies. Contact your university's international office for specific guidelines.

Can I convert my student visa to a work visa?+

Yes! After graduation, you can transition to a work visa if you find employment. We provide support for this transition, helping you and your employer navigate the process.

Living in São Paulo

What is the cost of living in São Paulo?+

Cost of living in São Paulo is high (comparable to major international cities).

Monthly expenses for a comfortable lifestyle typically include:

  • Rent: USD 500-1,500 depending on location
  • Food and groceries: USD 300-500
  • Utilities: USD 100-200
  • Transportation: USD 50-100
  • Entertainment: USD 100-300
What is the healthcare system like?+

Brazil has a comprehensive healthcare system with public (SUS) and private options:

  • Public Healthcare (SUS): Free for residents and citizens
  • Private Insurance: Affordable plans available
  • Quality: Modern facilities in major cities like São Paulo

International health insurance is recommended for comprehensive coverage.

What is the education system like for children?+

Brazil offers various educational options:

  • Public Schools: Free for all residents
  • Private Schools: International and Brazilian options available
  • Language: Portuguese is the medium of instruction; English options available

São Paulo has excellent schools. We can provide recommendations and enrollment assistance.

How do I open a bank account?+

Opening a bank account in Brazil is relatively easy:

  • Visit a Brazilian bank with your visa and identification
  • CPF (tax ID) helps but isn't always required for initial setup
  • Most banks offer accounts for foreigners
  • Online banking is available

We can provide guidance and bank recommendations in São Paulo.

Legal & Practical Questions

Do I need a police clearance?+

Yes, a police clearance (criminal background check) is required for most visa types. The certificate must be:

  • Issued within the last 3 months
  • Official government document
  • Translated into Portuguese by a certified translator

We can guide you on how to obtain this in your home country.

What if my visa application is denied?+

If your application is denied, you have options:

  • Request detailed reasons for the denial
  • File an administrative appeal (for some cases)
  • Reapply with corrected or additional documentation
  • Seek legal representation for complex cases

We specialize in appeals and can help you understand your options.

Do I need to speak Portuguese?+

While not strictly required for visa applications, Portuguese is essential for living in Brazil:

  • Business and work: Portuguese proficiency expected
  • Daily life: Helpful for shopping, healthcare, banking
  • Education: Child education typically in Portuguese

Many language schools operate in São Paulo. We recommend starting Portuguese lessons before or after arrival.

How do I get a CPF (Tax ID)?+

The CPF is essential for many activities in Brazil:

  • Request at the Federal Revenue Service (Receita Federal)
  • Online registration is available
  • You'll receive a certificate by mail
  • Needed for bank accounts, employment, and formal contracts

We can guide you through this process after you arrive in Brazil.

Can I extend my visa?+

Yes, most visas can be extended, depending on the visa type and circumstances. Extension procedures typically include:

  • Application to Federal Police
  • Required documentation (varies by visa type)
  • Processing time: typically 30-60 days

Contact us before your visa expires to start the extension process.
